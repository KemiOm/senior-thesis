\section{Effects of Hyperparameter Variation}

Across training rounds (R1--R4), the overall structure of results remains remarkably stable.
The heatmap shows nearly identical patterns across all rounds, with only small improvements in magnitude.

The primary quantitative difference arises from learning-rate variation.
Models trained with a learning rate of $1 \times 10^{-4}$ consistently outperform those trained with $5 \times 10^{-5}$ on task-specific exact match (Table~\ref{tab:lora-hyperparams-rounds}).
This suggests that the lower learning rate leads to underfitting, while the higher rate allows more effective adaptation of the LoRA parameters within the same training budget.

Other changes, including increased LoRA rank, additional attention modules, and longer sequence lengths, produce only marginal effects once supervision and eval are held fixed.
This indicates that model behavior is driven primarily by the supervision objective, not by fine-grained hyperparameter tuning.
